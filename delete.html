Data Deletion — Volatura

App: Volatura (com.volatura.app)
Developer: HornKru
Contact: volatura.app@gmail.com

This page explains how to delete the data associated with the Volatura app by HornKru, and what is deleted or retained. Volatura requires no account and does not collect your name or email, so most of your data lives on your own device or in your own Google Drive and is entirely under your control.

How to request or perform deletion

  1. Data on your device — Delete individual scores, annotations, setlists, or recordings from within the app, or uninstall Volatura to remove all of its on-device data at once.
  2. Your Google Drive backup (only if you enabled Cloud Backup) — In the app, open Settings → Cloud Backup, turn it off, and choose to delete the backup; or delete the app's data folder directly from your own Google Drive. This data is stored only in your Google account.
  3. Crash reports — In Settings, turn crash reporting off to stop any further collection. To request deletion of crash data already collected, email volatura.app@gmail.com.
  4. Feedback / bug reports you sent — To request deletion of a report you submitted, email volatura.app@gmail.com describing the report. We aim to action deletion requests within 30 days.

What is deleted and what is retained

DataWhere it livesDeletion / retention
Your library: PDFs, annotations, bookmarks, setlists, notes, recordings On your device Deleted when you delete the items or uninstall the app. Not retained by the developer.
Cloud backup of your library Your own Google Drive (private app folder) Removed when you delete the backup in the app or in your Drive. The developer cannot access it.
Crash reports (device model, OS, app version, stack trace) Firebase (developer) Only collected with your consent. Deleted on request; otherwise retained only as long as needed to fix the issue.
Feedback / bug reports (your message + device/app info) Firebase (developer) Sent only when you submit one. Contains no account identifier. Deleted on request; otherwise retained only as long as needed to resolve the issue.
Anonymous security identifier (abuse prevention) Firebase (developer) A random, non-identifying token; automatically expires and is not linked to you.
